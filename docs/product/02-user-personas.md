# User Personas

Talvorax serves anyone preparing for the job market. The platform explicitly distinguishes between "fresher" and "experienced" candidates in its interview setup, and its features map naturally to the personas below.

---

## Persona 1: The Fresh Graduate ("Fresher")

**Description**
A final-year student or recent graduate applying for their first full-time role. They have projects and coursework but little professional experience, and they have never faced a formal interview.

**Goals**
- Get their first resume past automated screening filters
- Learn what interviewers actually ask in their field
- Build confidence speaking under pressure

**Challenges**
- No idea how a recruiter or an ATS reads a resume
- Interview anxiety and no safe place to rehearse
- Frequent filler words and unstructured answers when speaking

**Why they use Talvorax**
They can upload their resume and immediately see a match score, missing skills, and concrete improvements. They can take beginner-friendly mock interviews tailored to their domain, hear the questions spoken aloud, answer by voice, and review where they lost points. Minute Talk gives them a daily, low-stakes way to practice speaking clearly.

---

## Persona 2: The Active Job Seeker

**Description**
A professional currently between roles or actively applying. They send many applications and prepare for interviews at multiple companies simultaneously.

**Goals**
- Tailor their resume to each target role quickly
- Prepare for interviews at specific companies
- Keep a steady stream of relevant job openings coming in

**Challenges**
- Rewriting the resume for every job posting is time-consuming
- Hard to judge which openings are realistically within reach
- Preparation effort is scattered across many tools

**Why they use Talvorax**
They paste a job description and get a scored analysis of how well their resume fits, then auto-optimize it and download a polished PDF. They set up job alerts by role, location, and skills, upload their resume once, and receive ranked recommendations with an honest shortlist verdict ("strong," "likely," "possible," "unlikely") plus tweak tips for each job. Company-specific mock interviews let them rehearse for particular employers.

---

## Persona 3: The Career Switcher

**Description**
A professional moving from one field into another — for example, from testing into data science, or from operations into product management.

**Goals**
- Understand which of their existing skills transfer
- Identify and close the gaps for the new domain
- Present their story convincingly in interviews

**Challenges**
- Their resume reads as belonging to the old career
- Uncertainty about what the new field requires
- Interview questions in the new domain feel unfamiliar

**Why they use Talvorax**
The resume analyzer can automatically detect their domain or let them target a new one, shows exactly which critical skills are missing for the target role, and suggests job roles that fit their current profile. The interview coach offers domain-based practice across eighteen fields with selectable topics, so they can rehearse the new material before facing a real panel. The market gap analysis in job matching shows which skills employers most often find missing from their profile.

---

## Persona 4: The Experienced Professional

**Description**
A candidate with several years of experience preparing for senior roles or interviews at top companies. They configure interviews with their actual years of experience so questions match their level.

**Goals**
- Refresh interview skills after years without interviewing
- Benchmark themselves before high-stakes conversations
- Polish an already strong resume for specific roles

**Challenges**
- Out of practice with interview formats and timed answers
- Needs harder, level-appropriate questions, not beginner material
- Limited time; wants targeted, efficient preparation

**Why they use Talvorax**
Interview setup lets them declare their experience level and years of experience so the questions are calibrated accordingly. They can revisit questions from past real interviews using the "Previous Interview Experience" mode, practice behavioral topics such as leadership and conflict resolution, and review recordings of their sessions. The dashboard tracks their scores across attempts so they can see whether they are ready.

---

## Persona 5: The Communication Improver

**Description**
A user whose main obstacle is spoken delivery — non-native speakers, nervous speakers, or anyone who wants to sound more fluent and confident.

**Goals**
- Reduce filler words and rambling
- Speak at a natural, confident pace
- Structure thoughts quickly on unfamiliar topics

**Challenges**
- No objective measure of how they actually sound
- Practice without feedback does not lead to improvement
- Anxiety about being judged while practicing

**Why they use Talvorax**
Minute Talk gives them a random topic and sixty seconds to speak while their words are transcribed live. Afterward, they see scores for content, structure, fluency, and confidence, their words-per-minute pace, a count of filler words with their most-used filler highlighted, and specific tips. Best score, last score, and total session counters make daily practice feel like a game.
