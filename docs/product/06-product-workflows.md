# Product Workflows

Step-by-step breakdowns of every major workflow in Talvorax.

---

## Workflow 1: Account Creation

```
Step 1: Open the sign-up page
        |
Step 2: Enter full name, email, password, and password confirmation
        (password must contain at least 8 characters with uppercase,
        lowercase, a number, and a special character)
        |
Step 3: Open and read the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
        in-app, then tick the agreement box
        |
Step 4: Submit — a confirmation email is sent
        |
Step 5: Click the link in the email to verify
        |
Final Result: verified account; the user logs in and lands
on the dashboard
```

---

## Workflow 2: Resume Analysis

```
Step 1: Upload a resume file (PDF, Word, or text);
        the content is extracted automatically
        |
Step 2: Pick the professional domain, or choose Auto Select
        and let the system detect it
        |
Step 3: Describe the target role in up to 50 words
        |
Step 4: Run the analysis
        |
Final Result: match score, ATS compatibility rating,
five-part score breakdown with evidence, missing critical
skills, actionable improvements, strengths and weaknesses,
and suggested job roles — saved to the account
```

---

## Workflow 3: Resume Optimization and Export

```
Step 1: (Optional) Click a suggested job role and review
        the skills required for it
        |
Step 2: (Optional) Build a skill list — click suggested
        skills to add them, remove any, or type new ones
        |
Step 3: Choose a template: Classic, Modern, or Minimal
        (preview any template full-page first)
        |
Step 4: Run Auto-Optimize — the resume content is rewritten
        for the target role, weaving in the selected skills
        |
Step 5: Preview the finished resume in the chosen layout
        |
Final Result: a polished, optimized resume downloaded as a PDF
```

---

## Workflow 4: Setting Up a Mock Interview

```
Step 1: Choose an interview mode
        (Domain, Job Description, Resume, Company Specific,
        or Previous Interview Experience)
        |
Step 2: Enter your name
        |
Step 3: Set experience level — fresher, or experienced with
        years of experience
        |
Step 4: Set length — 5 or 10 minutes, or 1-10 questions
        |
Step 5: Provide the mode-specific input:
        - Domain mode: domain + optional topic
        - JD mode: paste the job description
        - Resume mode: upload a PDF resume
        - Company mode: pick or enter a company
        - Previous Experience mode: past company, role,
          technical and behavioral topics, and optionally
          past questions and answers
        |
Final Result: a personalized question set is generated and
the live session begins
```

---

## Workflow 5: The Live Interview Session

```
Step 1: The camera starts and recording begins;
        the first question is read aloud with a greeting
        |
Step 2: The candidate answers out loud; speech is
        transcribed live on screen
        |
Step 3: A per-question countdown runs (up to 60 seconds,
        sized to each question's complexity)
        |
Step 4: The candidate moves on, skips, or is auto-advanced
        when time expires; questions can be bookmarked;
        answered and skipped questions are tracked
        |
Step 5: Throughout, the system watches the camera and warns
        if the face is not visible or if a second person
        appears, and flags non-English speech
        |
Step 6: After the final question, a closing message plays
        and the recording is finalized
        |
Final Result: the session is scored and the results
experience opens
```

---

## Workflow 6: Reviewing Interview Results

```
Step 1: Celebration screen — overall score and encouragement
        |
Step 2: Open the detailed analysis report
        |
Step 3: Browse the question list; each is labeled
        Good / Improve / Needs Work; filter to bookmarks
        |
Step 4: For each question, compare your transcribed answer
        with the ideal answer, side by side
        |
Step 5: Study the knowledge gaps map — topics ranked by
        average score, with subtopic tags to review
        |
Step 6: (Optional) Download the video recording
        |
Final Result: a personalized study plan; scores, takeaways,
and focus topics also flow into the dashboard summary
```

---

## Workflow 7: A Minute Talk Session

```
Step 1: Receive a random topic with category and difficulty
        (skip until one feels right)
        |
Step 2: Tap the microphone and start speaking
        |
Step 3: Speak for up to 60 seconds while the countdown runs
        and the transcript streams live
        |
Step 4: Stop early or let time expire; analysis runs
        automatically
        |
Final Result: overall score out of 100; content, structure,
fluency, and confidence sub-scores; words-per-minute pace
rating; filler-word count and top filler; actionable tips;
best/last/total counters update
```

---

## Workflow 8: Creating and Managing Job Alerts

```
Step 1: Open Job Alerts and click New Alert
        |
Step 2: Enter role title, optional location, remote-only
        preference, skills, and frequency
        (instant / daily / weekly)
        |
Step 3: Save — the alert appears in the list as active
        |
Step 4: Manage over time: pause or resume with a toggle,
        edit details, or delete
        |
Final Result: active alerts continuously match new job
listings, which are refreshed automatically every 6 hours
```

---

## Workflow 9: AI Job Matching

```
Step 1: In the AI Resume Match tab, upload a PDF resume
        (click or drag-and-drop, up to 10 MB)
        |
Step 2: The resume is parsed into a profile — name, contact,
        skills, target roles, seniority, years of experience —
        with a completeness percentage
        |
Step 3: The AI scores the profile against live job listings
        ("Force Sync Jobs" pulls the newest listings on demand)
        |
Step 4: Review ranked recommendation cards — match score,
        shortlist verdict, matched and missing skills, written
        reasoning, and per-job resume tweak tips
        |
Step 5: Filter by verdict (All / Strong / Likely / Possible)
        and sort by match score or recency
        |
Step 6: For each job: Apply Now on the original posting,
        Save for later, or Dismiss
        |
Final Result: a focused application shortlist plus a market
gap analysis of the skills most often missing across matches
```

---

## Workflow 10: Updating Account Settings

```
Step 1: Open Edit Profile from the profile menu
        |
Step 2: Update the display name and profile picture
        |
Step 3: To change email — enter the new address; verification
        links go to both the old and new inboxes
        |
Step 4: To change the password — confirm the current password,
        then set the new one
        |
Final Result: account details updated securely
```
