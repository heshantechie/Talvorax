# Use Cases

Practical, real-world scenarios showing how different users work with Talvorax. Each scenario uses only capabilities that exist in the product today.

---

## Use Case 1: A Final-Year Student Prepares Their First Resume

**User:** Priya, a final-year computer science student with projects but no work experience.

**Scenario:**
Priya signs up, accepts the terms, verifies her email, and lands on the dashboard, which suggests starting with a resume analysis. She uploads her resume as a PDF and, unsure how to classify herself, chooses "Auto Select" so the system detects her domain. She types "frontend developer with React and JavaScript" as her target keywords.

Her match score comes back low. The report shows her project descriptions lack measurable impact, and two critical skills from her target role are missing. She clicks the suggested role "Frontend Developer," adds the recommended skills she genuinely has, picks the Modern template, runs Auto-Optimize, previews the result, and downloads a clean PDF.

**Outcome:** Priya applies with a professionally formatted, role-targeted resume and knows which two skills to build next.

## Use Case 2: A Fresher Faces Their First Mock Interview

**User:** Arjun, a recent graduate who has never been interviewed.

**Scenario:**
Arjun opens the Interview Coach and picks Domain Based mode, selecting "Frontend Development" with the topic "React." He marks himself as a fresher and chooses a short interview of five questions. The session greets him by name and reads the first question aloud. His camera is on; when he leans out of frame to check notes, an alert reminds him to stay visible — just like a real remote interview.

He fumbles two answers and skips one. On the results screen his overall score appears with an encouraging message, and the detailed report shows his transcribed answers next to ideal answers. The knowledge gaps map flags "State Management" as his weakest topic.

**Outcome:** Arjun studies the flagged topics, retakes the interview two days later, and watches his dashboard score improve.

## Use Case 3: An Active Job Seeker Targets a Specific Posting

**User:** Meera, a marketing professional applying to a specific role she found online.

**Scenario:**
Meera pastes the role's key requirements into the Resume Analyzer with the Marketing domain selected and sees a moderate score with a warning that a required skill is missing entirely — triggering the report's gatekeeper penalty. She uses the actionable improvements to reframe two bullet points, adds the missing skill she actually has but had not mentioned, and exports an optimized resume in the Classic template.

Before the interview, she uses the Interview Coach's Job Description Based mode, pasting the same posting so the questions mirror the role.

**Outcome:** Meera applies with a resume tuned to that exact posting and has rehearsed the interview it is likely to produce.

## Use Case 4: A Career Switcher Maps the Gap

**User:** Suresh, a QA engineer moving into data science.

**Scenario:**
Suresh runs his resume through the analyzer with the Data Science domain. The report shows which of his skills transfer and lists missing critical skills. In the Job Alerts area, he uploads his resume, and the AI Resume Match tab scores him against live listings. Most verdicts come back "Possible" rather than "Strong," and the Market Gap Analysis panel shows the same two skills missing across nearly every matched job.

He sets up a daily job alert for "Data Analyst" roles as a stepping stone, saves the strongest matches, and dismisses the unrealistic ones. For each saved job he opens the resume tweak tips and adjusts his resume accordingly.

**Outcome:** Suresh has a data-driven upskilling list, a realistic target level for his switch, and a managed pipeline of transition-friendly openings.

## Use Case 5: An Experienced Professional Rehearses for a Top Company

**User:** Kavitha, a backend engineer with seven years of experience interviewing at a large multinational.

**Scenario:**
Kavitha selects Company Specific mode, picks the company from the list, sets herself as experienced with seven years, and chooses a ten-minute session. The questions arrive at her level. She bookmarks the two questions that surprised her. Afterward, she downloads the video recording to review her body language and filters the report to her bookmarked questions to study the ideal answers.

She also uses Previous Interview Experience mode to re-run questions from an interview she failed last year, entering the company, the role, and the questions she remembered, with "System Design" and "Conflict Resolution" as her chosen topics.

**Outcome:** Kavitha walks into the real interview having already faced the company's question style and having fixed the exact answers that cost her last time.

## Use Case 6: A Nervous Speaker Builds Fluency

**User:** Rahul, a strong engineer whose interviews fall apart when he speaks.

**Scenario:**
Rahul opens Minute Talk each morning. Today's topic: "How is remote work changing the modern workplace?" — Career category, Medium difficulty. He taps the microphone and speaks for the full sixty seconds while his words stream on screen. His report shows an overall 58: fluency 5 out of 10, 21 filler words with "like" as his top filler, and a pace of 95 words per minute labeled "Too Slow."

He follows the actionable tips, skips to a new topic, and tries again. Over three weeks, his Best Score card climbs from 58 to 84 and his filler count drops to single digits.

**Outcome:** Rahul's delivery catches up with his knowledge, and the stat cards give him proof of progress.

## Use Case 7: A Busy Professional Automates Discovery

**User:** Anita, employed full-time but open to better opportunities.

**Scenario:**
Anita creates two alerts: a weekly alert for "Product Manager" roles in her city, and an instant alert for remote-only roles matching her top skills. She uploads her resume once so every synchronized job is scored against her actual profile. Once a week she opens the recommendations, filters to "Strong," and applies to the top matches through the direct links — saving the "Likely" ones for later.

When she gets busy, she pauses the alerts with one toggle and resumes them a month later.

**Outcome:** High-fit opportunities find Anita instead of the other way around, with her total search time reduced to minutes per week.

## Use Case 8: A Returning User Tracks Improvement

**User:** Vikram, who has completed six mock interviews over a month.

**Scenario:**
Vikram logs in and his dashboard now shows Recent Interview Performance: his last score on a gauge, his three skill areas averaged across all six sessions with Problem Solving ranked weakest, focus topics collected from every interview, and key takeaways summarizing his recurring feedback. The average across attempts sits at the bottom of the summary card.

He clicks "Take another interview," picks Resume Based mode, and uploads his newly optimized resume so the questions target what he now claims.

**Outcome:** Vikram's preparation is a closed loop — practice, measure, study, repeat — with the dashboard as scoreboard.
